Intro to statistical research methods

Kinds of studies

Observational Experimental
Experimenter only observes, does not introduce any treatment. Experimenter introduce treatments to manipulate explanatory variables and measure response variables.
  • establish correlation
  • unless using causal inference
  • retrospective: use past data
  • prospective: data collected throughout the study
  • show causation
  • randomly assign subjects to treatments

principles of experimental design

1. Control compare treatment of interest to a control group
2. Randomize randomly assign subjects to treatments
3. Replicate collect a sufficiently large sample, or replicate the entire study
4. Blocking block for variables known or suspect to affect the outcome

Results validity factors

Here are some of the most relevant factors that influence the validity of a research's results:

  1. Sample size: good sample size is needed to infer valid conclusions
  2. Representative sample: who i surveyed
  3. Solid methodology: how the survey was conducted

Experimentation definitions

Treatment

The factor (also called an independent variable) is an explanatory variable manipulated by the experimenter. Each factor has two or more levels, i.e., different values of the factor. Combinations of factor levels are called treatments

Independent variable

A.k.a explanatory variable, is the variable that experimenters choose to manipulate, because it's thought to be responsible for direct changes in the system under study.

Dependent variable

A.k.a response variable, is the variable that experimenters choose to measure during an experiment, because it reflects the state of the system under study.

Blocking variables

Variables that may affect the measured result, but are not of primary interest. Characteristics that the experimental units come with, e.g. gender. Taken into account to stratify the experiment can contribute to decrease bias.

When we can control nuisance factors, an important technique known as blocking can be used to reduce or eliminate the contribution to experimental error contributed by nuisance factors.

Treatment group

The group of a study that receives varying levels of the independent variable. These groups are used to measure the effect of a treatment.

Control group

The group of a study that receives no treatment. This group is used as a baseline when comparing treatment groups.

Placebo

Something given to subjects in the control group so they think they are getting the treatment, when in reality they are getting something that causes no effect to them.

Blinding

Blinding is a technique used to reduce bias. Double blinding ensures that both those administering treatments and those receiving treatments do not know who is receiving which treatment
