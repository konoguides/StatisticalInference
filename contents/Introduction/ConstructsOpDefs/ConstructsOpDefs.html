Constructs & types of variables

Constructs

Construct

An explanatory variable which is not directly observable, and thus difficult to measure because it can be defined and measured in many different ways.

The concepts of intelligence and motivation are used to explain phenomena in psychology, but neither is directly observable

Operational definitions

Operational definition

Precise description of how to measure a construct. In practice it is the unit of measurement we are using for the construct.

It's important to remember that any measure of a construct is simply a proposal; but once we operationally define something it is no longer a construct.

Types of variables

Quantitative (numerical)
continuous usually measured; can take any numerical value; we tend to round while recording
discrete generally counted; whole, non-negative numbers
Qualitative (categorical)
  • take on a limited number of distinct categories
  • this categories can be identified with numbers (e.g. 0 - male, 1 - female)
ordinal have ordered levels
regular categorical do not have inherent ordering
