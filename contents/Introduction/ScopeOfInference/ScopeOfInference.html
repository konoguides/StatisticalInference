Scope of inference

  • refers to the type of conclusions (inferences) that can be drawn from a study
  • inferences about population (generalizable) and about causes-and-effect
  • determined by two factors in the design of the study:
    • random sampling how subjects were selected from the population
    random assignment how subjects were assigned to groups, implemented to try making groups similar and reduce the influence of uncontrol variables
randomness contributes to reduce the impact of uncontrolled variables
