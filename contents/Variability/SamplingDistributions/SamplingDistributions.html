Sampling distributions

Sampling distribution

Is the probability distribution of a statistic computed for each possible sample of size \(n\) from a given population of size \(N\).

Standard error (SE)

The standard deviation of the sampling distribution. It is related with the population standard deviation \(\left( \sigma \right)\) as: $$\sigma = \sqrt{n} \cdot SE \hspace{1cm} \rightarrow \hspace{1cm} SE = \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}$$ where \(n\) is the size of the samples associated with the sampling distribution.

Variability of sampling distribution

  • measured by its variance of its standard deviation
  • depends on three factors
    • \(N\): number of observations in the population
    • \(n\): number of observations in the sample
    • the way that the random sample is chosen

Central limit theorem

Central limit (simplified)

The sampling distribution of the mean of any independent, random variable will be normal or nearly normal, if the sample size is large enough.

  • regardless of whether the population distribution is normal or not
  • help us understand the following facts:
    1. mean of the sample means is the same as the population mean
    2. standard deviation of the sample means is always equal to the standard error
    3. distribution of sample means will become more normal as sample size \(n\), increases
