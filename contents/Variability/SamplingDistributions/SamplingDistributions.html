Sampling distribution

Is the probability distribution of a statistic computed for each possible sample of size \(n\) from a given population of size \(N\).

Standard error (SE)

The standard deviation of the sampling distribution. Measures its variability, and tell us the interval in which the population mean is most likely going to lie. And it's related with the population standard deviation \(\left( \sigma \right)\) as: $$\sigma = \sqrt{n} \cdot SE \hspace{1cm} \rightarrow \hspace{1cm} SE = \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}$$ where \(n\) is the number of observations in the samples associated with the sampling distribution.

Applications of sampling distributions

  • determine where in the sampling distribution a particular sample lies
  • if know where it falls, we can decide in sample is typical or something rear is going on
  • sample location description: how many \(SE\) away from center of sampling distribution $$\text{z-score} = \frac{\bar{x} - \mu}{SE}$$

Central limit theorem

Central limit (simplified)

The sampling distribution of the mean of any independent, random variable will be normal or nearly normal, if the sample size is large enough.

  • regardless of whether the population distribution is normal or not
  • help us understand the following facts:
    1. mean of the sample means is the same as the population mean
    2. standard deviation of the sample means is always equal to the standard error
    3. distribution of sample means will become more normal as sample size \(n\), increases

Finite population correction (fpc) for the SE

  • when population size \(\left( N \right)\) very large relative to sample size \(\left( n \right)\): \(fpc \rightarrow 1\)
  • standard error can be approximated by: \(SE = \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}\)
  • safe to use when \(n < \frac{1}{20} N\)
  • otherwise use the finite population correction factor: $$SE = \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}} \cdot \sqrt{\frac{N-n}{N-1}}$$
