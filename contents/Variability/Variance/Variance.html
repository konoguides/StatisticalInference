Variance

The variance of a random variable is a measure of spread of a density arround its mean. It is is defined as the expected square distance the random variable is from the mean: $$Var\left(X\right) = \sigma^2 = E\left[\left(X-\mu\right)^2\right] = E\left[X^2\right] - E\left[X\right]^2$$

  • densities with higher variance are more apread than those with lower
  • variance is always non-negative
  • small variance = data points tend to be very close to the mean to each other
  • variance of zero indicates that all the values are identical

Standard deviation

  • square root of the variance $$\sigma = \sqrt{\sigma^2}$$
