Bayes rule

Bayes' Theorem

  • allows to reverse the role of events involved in conditional probability
  • relates \(P(B|A)\) with \(P(A|B)\)
  • semantically: rule incorporates new evidence into prior probability to arrive a posterior probability
  • given that if \(A\) and \(B\) are not independent:

$$\begin{equation} P(A | B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)} \label{eq:one} \end{equation}$$ and, $$\begin{equation} P(B | A) = \frac{P(B \cap A)}{P(A)} = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(A)}, \label{eq:two} \end{equation}$$ from \eqref{eq:one} (join probability) we an obtain: $$\begin{equation} P(A \cap B) = P(B, A) = P(A | B) * P(B) \label{eq:three} \end{equation}$$ and substitude it on \eqref{eq:two}, we get: $$\begin{equation} P(B | A) = \frac{P(B)}{P(A)} P(A | B) \label{eq:four} \end{equation}$$ Please not that \(P(A)\) can be rewritted as: $$\begin{equation} P(A) = P(A \cap B) + P(A \cap B^c) \label{eq:five} \end{equation}$$ using \eqref{eq:three} on \eqref{eq:five} we have: $$\begin{equation} P(A) = P(A | B) * P(B) + P(A | B^c) * P(B^c) \label{eq:six} \end{equation}$$ finally changing \(P(A)\) from \eqref{eq:four} by \eqref{eq:six}, we end up with the Baye's rule, which is the posterior probability, where the denominator \(P(A)\) corresponds to the normalizer because it represents the total probability of the \(A\) event

$$P(B|A) = \frac{P(A|B) * P(B)}{P(A|B) * P(B)+P(A|B^c) * P(B^c)}$$

where:

  • \(P(A|B^c) = 1 - P(A^c|B^c)\)
  • \(P(B^c) = 1 - P(B)\)
