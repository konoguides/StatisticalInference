Kown \(\sigma\) confidence intervals

How to construct a confidence interval

1. Choose the statistic
  • statistic that you will use to estimate a population parameter
2. Select a confidence level (c)
  • any percentage
  • most commons 90%, 95% and 99%
  • \(c=1- \alpha\)
3. Find the margin of error (me) $$\begin{array}{ccl} \text{me} & = & \text{critical value} \cdot \text{std of sampling distribution} \\ & = & z^* \cdot SE \\ & & \\ z^* & = & CDF^{-1}_{\mathscr{N}(0,1)} \left( \frac{1+c}{2} \right) \end{array}$$
4. Specify the confidence interval $$\begin{array}{rcl} CI & = & \text{sample statistic} \pm \text{margin of error} \\ & = & \text{sample statistic} \pm z^* \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}} \end{array}$$ where \(n\) is the size of sample used to construct the CI.
Two-tailed interval 
>>> from scipy.stats import norm
>>> import numpy as np

>>> c = 0.98                    # confidence level
>>> sample_stat = 40.0          # e.g. mean
>>> sigma = 16.06
>>> n = 250.0

# USING INTERVAL METHOD OF CONTINUOS DISTRIBUTIONS
>>> norm.interval(c, loc=sample_stat, scale=sigma/np.sqrt(n))
(37.637073598710714, 42.362926401289286)
                        
# MANUAL CALCULATION
>>> z_char = norm.ppf((1+c)/2.0)
>>> me = z_char * sigma / np.sqrt(n)
>>> print '({}, {})'.format(sample_stat - me, sample_stat + me)
(37.6370735987, 42.3629264013)
