Unknown \(\sigma\) confidence intervals

How to construct a confidence interval

1. Choose the statistic
  • statistic that you will use to estimate a population parameter
2. Select a confidence level (c)
  • any percentage
  • most commons 90%, 95% and 99%
  • \(c=1- \alpha\)
3. Find the margin of error (me) $$\begin{array}{ccl} \text{me} & = & \text{critical value} \cdot \text{std of sampling distribution} \\ & = & t^* \cdot SE \\ & & \\ t^* & = & CDF^{-1}_{t(r)} \left( \frac{1+c}{2} \right) \end{array}$$ where \(r = n-1\) are the dof for the Student t distribution
4. Specify the confidence interval $$\begin{array}{rcl} CI & = & \text{sample statistic} \pm \text{margin of error} \\ & = & \text{sample statistic} \pm t^* \frac{s}{\sqrt{n}} \end{array}$$ where \(n\) is the size of sample used to construct the CI.
Two-tailed interval 
>>> from scipy.stats import norm
>>> import numpy as np

>>> c = 0.95                    # confidence level
>>> sample_stat = 180.0         # e.g. mean
>>> s = 30.0
>>> n = 1000.0

# USING INTERVAL METHOD OF CONTINUOS DISTRIBUTIONS
>>> t.interval(c, n-1, sample_stat, s/np.sqrt(n))
(178.13835943075065, 181.86164056924935)
                        
# MANUAL CALCULATION
>>> t_char = t.ppf(0.5*(1+c), n-1)
>>> me = t_char * s / np.sqrt(n)
>>> print '({}, {})'.format(sample_stat - me, sample_stat + me)
(178.138359431, 181.861640569)
