Unknown standard deviation confidence intervals

How to construct a confidence interval

  1. Choose the statistic that you will use to estimate a population parameter
  2. Select a confidence level \(\left(c = \left(1 - \alpha\right) \right) \): any percentage, most commons 90%, 95% and 99%
  3. Find the margin of error: based on unknown standard deviation \(s\) $$\begin{array}{ccl} \text{margin of error} & = & \text{critical value} \cdot \text{std of sampling distribution} \\ & = & t^* \cdot SE \end{array}$$ with: $$t^* = CDF^{-1}_{t(r)} \left( \frac{1+c}{2} \right)$$ where \(r = n-1\) are the degrees of freedom for the Student t distribution
  4. Specify the confidence interval: $$\begin{array}{rcl} CI & = & \text{sample statistic} \pm \text{margin of error} \\ & = & \text{sample statistic} \pm t^* \frac{s}{\sqrt{n}} \end{array}$$ where \(n\) is the size of sample used to construct the CI.
