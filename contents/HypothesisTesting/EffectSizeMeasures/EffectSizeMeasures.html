Effect size measures

Effect size

  • size of the treatment effect in experimental studies
  • strength in relationship between variables in non-experimental studies

Effect size measures

  • mean difference → excellent measure when dealing with easy-to-understand variables
  • not a good choice in cases when we don't understand what the variable is telling us
  • other effect size measures has benn developed

Types of effect size measures

  • divided into two families
Difference measures
Mean difference
  • simplest measure
  • excelent when easy-to-understand variables
  • \(\bar{x} - \mu\)
Cohen's d
  • a standarizd differences measure
  • tell us about mean difference but in std units
  • how far, in SD, are the means
    • $$d = \frac{\bar{x} - \mu}{s}$$
Correlation measures
R2 - coefficient of determination
  • proportion of variation in one variable that is related to another
  • strength of relationship between variables
  • ranges from \(0.00\) to \(1.00\)
    • $$R^2 = \frac{t^2}{t^2 + \text{dof}}$$ where \(t = \frac{\bar{x} - \mu}{SE}\) and \(\text{dof} = n-1\)
